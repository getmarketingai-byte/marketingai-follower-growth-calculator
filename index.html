What Is Follower Growth Rate?

Follower growth rate measures how fast your social media audience is expanding. It's expressed as a percentage and shows whether your social strategy is attracting new followers over a specific time period.

Formula: Growth Rate = ((Ending Followers - Starting Followers) / Starting Followers) × 100

For Australian SMBs, a healthy monthly growth rate sits between 3.0% - 5.0% across most platforms and industries. This translates to roughly 36% - 60% annualised growth, though rates vary significantly by platform (TikTok and Instagram tend to see faster growth than LinkedIn) and content niche.

A slow growth rate usually signals one of three issues: inconsistent posting, weak content strategy, or limited audience targeting. The good news is that all three are completely fixable with the right approach.

If your monthly growth is consistently below 1%, it's worth auditing your posting frequency, content relevance, and hashtag strategy. If you're above 5%, you're outperforming most Australian SMBs -- focus next on converting followers into customers.
